FOSS: Free and Open Source Software

What is FOSS?

FOSS is software that can be freely used, modified, and distributed by anyone. Its source code is available to anyone who wants to study or modify it.

Benefits over proprietary software

  • Freedom to use the software for any purpose.
  • Freedom to study and modify the source code to suit your needs.
  • Freedom to distribute the software to others.
  • Transparency and security due to the availability of the source code.
  • Community support and collaboration.

History of FOSS development

FOSS has its roots in the early days of computing, but it really took off in the 1980s and 1990s with the advent of the GNU project and the Linux operating system. Today, FOSS is used in everything from servers and desktops to mobile devices and embedded systems.

FOSS alternatives to common applications

  • LibreOffice - office suite
  • GIMP - image editing software
  • Firefox - web browser
  • Ubuntu - operating system

FOSS ties with GNU/Linux

FOSS and GNU/Linux go hand in hand, as many of the core components of a typical Linux distribution are open source. This allows users to customize and modify their systems to suit their needs, and to contribute back to the community.
